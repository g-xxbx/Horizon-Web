## [New York City bans deceptive subscription practices](https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/2026/jul/10/new-york-city-deceptive-subscriptions-ban) ⭐️ 8.0/10

New York City has become the first city in the US to pass legislation banning deceptive subscription practices, including dark patterns and hidden junk fees, with a 'click to cancel' requirement. This landmark consumer protection policy targets the tech industry's widespread use of subscription models that trick users, potentially setting a precedent for other cities and states to follow. The law requires companies to allow subscribers to cancel with equal ease as signing up, without requiring a phone call or in-person visit, and prohibits hidden fees that inflate the final price.

hackernews · randycupertino · Jul 10, 18:26 · [Discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48863464)

**Background**: Dark patterns are user interface designs intentionally crafted to trick users into taking actions they don't intend, such as making it easy to subscribe but hard to cancel. Junk fees, also known as hidden fees, are mandatory charges that are not disclosed upfront, obscuring the true cost of a product or service. Together, these practices have become common in subscriptions for software, media, and services, prompting growing regulatory scrutiny.

## [China's Long March 10B achieves world-first net-based sea recovery](https://weibo.com/7340734455/R814of1Ki) ⭐️ 8.0/10

On July 10, the Long March 10B rocket launched from Hainan Commercial Space Launch Site. Its first stage separated, returned vertically, and was captured by a net system on a sea platform, marking China's first controlled rocket recovery and the world's first net-based recovery. This milestone in reusable rocket technology demonstrates China's growing capability in aerospace engineering. Net-based recovery could simplify rocket design, reduce weight, and lower launch costs, benefiting satellite constellation deployment and deep space missions. The recovery used the specialized platform 'Linghangzhe' (Navigator), which employs multi-sensor fusion and feedforward surge compensation to maintain positioning accuracy within 0.5 meters in 4-meter waves. Net-based capture eliminates the need for landing legs, reducing structural weight and increasing payload capacity.

telegram · zaihuapd · Jul 10, 04:36

**Background**: Rocket stage recovery involves returning the first stage after separation for reuse, reducing launch costs. The most common method is propulsive landing with landing legs, as used by SpaceX's Falcon 9. Net-based recovery uses a flexible net on a sea platform to capture the stage, which can be gentler and save weight. China had not previously performed a controlled recovery of an orbital rocket stage.
