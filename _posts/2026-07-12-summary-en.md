## [GPT-5.6 Proves 50-Year-Old Graph Conjecture in Under an Hour](https://www.qbitai.com/2026/07/447873.html) ⭐️ 10.0/10

OpenAI's GPT-5.6 Sol Ultra model has reportedly proven the cycle double cover conjecture, a long-standing open problem in graph theory, in less than one hour. The proof was generated using a parallel agent architecture with 64 sub-agents and produced a 3-page PDF. This achievement demonstrates the potential of large language models to make significant contributions to mathematical research, especially in solving open problems that have resisted human effort for decades. It could accelerate progress in graph theory and inspire new approaches to AI-assisted reasoning in mathematics and science. The model transformed the conjecture into a problem of edge labeling over finite fields and solving linear equations, using 64 parallel sub-agents to explore different parts of the proof. OpenAI also released the full prompt, which specifies acceptance criteria, definitions, and boundary conditions rather than fixed steps.

telegram · zaihuapd · Jul 12, 03:49

**Background**: The cycle double cover conjecture posits that every bridgeless undirected graph has a collection of cycles that together contain each edge exactly twice. It was independently formulated by Szekeres in 1973 and Seymour in 1979, and has remained unsolved until this claimed proof. GPT-5.6's approach involves a parallel agent architecture where multiple sub-agents work simultaneously on different aspects of the problem, coordinated by a main agent. The model's ability to reason about mathematical structures and generate a valid proof represents a major leap in AI capabilities.

## [World's First Invasive Brain-Computer Interface Medical Device Approved](https://t.me/zaihuapd/42515) ⭐️ 10.0/10

China's National Medical Products Administration has approved the 'Implantable Brain-Computer Interface Hand Motor Function Compensation System' developed by Braincore Medical Technology (Shanghai) Co., Ltd., marking the world's first invasive brain-computer interface medical device to enter clinical application. This approval represents a major breakthrough from laboratory research to clinical practice, offering a new therapeutic option for patients with spinal cord injuries. It could significantly improve the hand grasping ability and quality of life for quadriplegic patients. The system employs epidural minimally invasive implantation and wireless power supply and communication technology, and is indicated for patients aged 18 to 60 with quadriplegia due to cervical spinal cord injury. Clinical trial results demonstrated notable improvement in hand grasping function.

telegram · zaihuapd · Jul 12, 14:39

**Background**: Invasive brain-computer interfaces (BCIs) involve implanting electrodes directly into the brain cortex or epidural space to record neural signals with high resolution. This approach offers better signal quality compared to non-invasive BCIs but carries risks such as infection and surgical complications. The epidural implantation technique used in this device places electrodes outside the dura mater, minimizing direct brain tissue damage while maintaining high signal-to-noise ratio. China has been advancing its BCI technology, with previous clinical trials conducted by research institutions.

George Hotz published a blog post arguing that LLMs create substantial value, but frontier AI labs may fail to capture that value, while real productivity gains occur in private, customized software. This critique from a prominent hacker challenges the high valuations of frontier labs and highlights a shift toward decentralized, user-driven AI applications, with broad implications for open-source sustainability and AI economics. Commenters note that subscription prices for frontier models are still subsidized, and many users are building one-off custom software with LLMs, raising concerns about the future of upstreaming in open-source projects.

## [xAI Grok CLI Uploads Entire Codebase and Secret Files by Default](https://gist.github.com/cereblab/dc9a40bc26120f4540e4e09b75ffb547) ⭐️ 8.0/10

A security researcher discovered that xAI's official Grok Build CLI (version 0.2.93) automatically uploads the entire code repository and secret files (like .env) to xAI servers via two channels, even when the 'improve model' setting is disabled. This vulnerability poses a serious privacy and data leakage risk for developers using Grok CLI, as entire codebases and sensitive credentials could be exposed to xAI without user consent, undermining trust in AI coding tools. The analysis focused on Grok Build version 0.2.93 and showed that a 12 GB repository resulted in over 5 GiB of data uploaded without server rejection. The 'improve model' toggle was ineffective, and the researcher confirmed data transmission and storage but found no evidence of use for model training.

telegram · zaihuapd · Jul 12, 04:19

**Background**: The Grok CLI is a command-line tool by xAI that provides access to Grok AI models for coding assistance, capable of reading files and executing tasks in the terminal. A git bundle is a single-file representation of a git repository containing all objects and references, often used for offline transfer. This finding raises concerns about data privacy in AI-powered developer tools.
